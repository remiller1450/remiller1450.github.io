Non-parametric Models (part I - kNN)

Ryan Miller

Parametric vs. Non-parametric models

\[Y = f(\mathbf{X}) + \epsilon\]

  • Parametric methods, such as linear or logistic regression (and variants like LASSO and GAM) specific a particular structure for \(f\), the function describing how \(\mathbf{X}\) is related to \(Y\)
    • Often these methods provide high interpretability, but low flexibility
  • Non-parametric methods, do not impose any particular structure on \(f\), instead applying an algorithm for generating an \(\hat{f}\)
    • These methods often provide high flexibility

The k-nearest Neighbors Algorithm

k-nn regression:

  • Predict \(Y_i\) as the average \(Y\) value of the \(k\) nearest data-points

k-nn classification:

  • Predict \(Y_i\) as the majority class of the \(k\) nearest data-points

Conclusion

  • k-nearest neighbors is an easy to understand non-parametric model
    • It is more flexible than methods like linear and logistic regression
    • However, it tends to overfit and often performs worse than the regression-based methods
