In-class Labs

Throughout the semester we will practice applying key concepts in applications involving real data during in-class labs. During labs, you will work collaboratively in a group of 2-3 students to progress through a guided data analysis. You will have the majority of class to work independently with your group, and we will will often go over a few key questions towards the end of class. Your group will be expected to turn-in a lab write-up.

  • Week 1: Data basics and an introduction to R
  • Week 2: Data visualization
  • Week 3: none (Exam #1 on Thursday)

Exam Dates and Information

There will be 4 midterm exams covering the following topics:

  • Exam 1 - Descriptive statistics, graphical presentations, and study design (Ch 1 and 2)
  • Exam 2 - Probability and random variables (Ch 3 and 4)
  • Exam 3 - Statistical inference for one-sample data (Ch 5, 6.1, and 7.1)
  • Exam 4 - statistical inference for two or more samples (remaining sections of Ch 6, 7, and 8)

You will be given an entire class period to complete each exam, though I will try to write them to target an average completion time of around 60-minutes. With the exception of Exam 2, all exams will “open everything”, and will require you to use R. Exam 2 will be on-paper and closed notes (a small set of formulas will be given).
