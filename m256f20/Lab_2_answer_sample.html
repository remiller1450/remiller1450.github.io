## Example 1
ggplot(colleges19, aes(x = Salary10yr_median, y = State)) + geom_boxplot()

## Example #2
ggplot(colleges19, aes(x = Debt_median, y = Salary10yr_median)) + geom_point()

## Example #3
ggplot(colleges19, aes(x = State, fill = Private)) + geom_bar(position = "fill")

  1. Median salaries tend to vary according to the state a college is located in.
  2. There appears to be a positive association between a schools median student debt and its students future salary.
  3. Private schools are more common in most states, but some states like WY, AK, etc. only have public schools.

## Example 1
ggplot(colleges19, aes(x = Salary10yr_median, y = State)) + geom_boxplot() + facet_wrap(~Private)

## Example #2
ggplot(colleges19, aes(x = Debt_median, y = Salary10yr_median, col = Private)) + geom_point() 

## Example #3 - Note - I used a logical condition to create a categorical variable here
ggplot(colleges19, aes(x = State, fill = Private)) + geom_bar(position = "fill") + facet_wrap(~Enrollment > 5000)

  1. After stratifying we see private and public schools have very different salaries, but it doesnt necessarily seem to be influencing the comparison across states.
  2. The relationship between debt and salary doesnt seem much different for between private and public schools, though the correlation might be slightly stronger for private schools.
  3. School size is strongly related to whether a school is private or public, which larger schools tending to be public.
